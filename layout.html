There are many different kinds of pandas, but the most common panda is the giant panda. The species is native to South Central China, where it consumes bamboo shoots and leaves for nutrition. Giant pandas in the wild also occasionally eat different grasses, wild tubers or even smaller animals.

Giant pandas are characterized by its black-and-white coat and rotund body where they have black spots on the ears, eye patches, muzzle, legs, arms and shoulders. Their coat is speculated to bring them protection in their heavily shaded environment, as most predators are colorblind. They have the body shape of a typical bear.
